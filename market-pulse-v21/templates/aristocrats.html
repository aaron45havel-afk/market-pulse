Why yield-vs-history instead of 52-week lows? And how the filters work
  • Value: an aristocrat's dividend is reliable and growing, so its yield mean-reverts. Current yield ≥{{ premium_min|int }}% above the stock's own 5-yr median = historically cheap, no matter what the price chart did. A 52-week low is momentum, not valuation — the list goes empty in rallies, floods in crashes, and catches falling knives (Leggett & Platt sat at 52-week lows for years before cutting after 52 straight raises). We show % off the 52-week high as context, never as the trigger.
  • Return (+12%): the Chowder number — current yield + 5-yr dividend CAGR — approximates long-run total return if the valuation holds. Hurdle {{ hurdle|int }} for most names, {{ hurdle_low|int }} for utilities/REITs/midstream per the standard rule.
  • Safety: payout ≥80% or net debt/EBITDA ≥3.5× hides the name (GATED). Elevated-but-passing names keep a warning badge — Altria stays visible, you decide.
  • International: streaks are in local currency. The raise streak can be intact while your USD payment wobbles with FX — that's the FX badge. OTC tickers are thin; use limit orders on Schwab.
