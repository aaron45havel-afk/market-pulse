How does Multifamily Score work? (FHA-tuned)
ZIP scores are percentile-blended within the selected state — "best in {{ state }}", not against the whole country. Weighted toward what matters for a small-and-mighty FHA owner-occupant. {% if has_mf_data %}
  • House-hack net cost (35%) — your monthly PITI minus rent collected from the other units. Lower is better.
  • Cap rate (25%) — cash flow at current local rents.
  • % renter-occupied (20%) — bigger tenant pool, faster fills.
  • % multi-unit housing (10%) — existing density signals an active market with 2-4 unit stock to actually buy.
  • Rent burden inverted (10%) — lower share of cost-burdened renters = more stable tenants.
{% else %}
  • House-hack net cost (40%) — your monthly PITI minus rent collected from the other units. Lower is better.
  • Cap rate (35%) — cash flow at current local rents.
  • Population density (15%) — proxy for renter share until ACS data lands.
  • Walk score (10%) — walkable urban form correlates with renter preference.
{% endif %}

House-hack math: purchase = min(local median home value, your cap). PITI = P&I + state-level property tax + state-level insurance + FHA MIP (0.55% annual, life-of-loan when down < 10%). Expected rent = (units − 1) × local median rent. Net monthly cost = PITI − expected rent. Negative = you live free + cash flow.

FHA self-sufficiency (3- and 4-unit only): FHA requires 75% × total rents ≥ PITI for the loan to fund. Two-unit purchases are exempt from this rule. We mark FHA on rows that pass, FHA ✗ on rows that fail at your input price.

Structural lens (new):

  • 3-yr trend — annualized home-value change from the ZIP's own 60-month history, with a second-derivative read: ▲ accelerating · → steady · ⚠ decelerating · ▼ declining.
  • Trajectory veto — a declining ZIP's score is capped (at 79/69/59 depending on how many state-level structural flags are active); a decelerating ZIP loses 5 points. Vetoed rows show capped so a cheap-but-shrinking market can't top the table on level alone.
  • Durable cap — gross yield × 92% (vacancy) − state property tax − state insurance ÷ home value − 1.5% maintenance reserve. A fixed insurance premium is a big % drag on a $60k duplex and a rounding error on a $500k one — this column is where that honesty lives.
  • Stressed — house-hack net if rents come in 10% light, insurance reprices +30%, and you eat one vacant month a year. If this still cash-flows, the deal survives a structural shift.

ZIPs with population <1,500 or median home value >1.2× your cap are excluded. Capped at top 100 results.
