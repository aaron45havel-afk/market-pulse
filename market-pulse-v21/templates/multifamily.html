How does Multifamily Score work?
A 0–100 percentile-blended score across all ZIPs in the selected state. Each input ranks the ZIP against its in-state peers, then signals are weighted-averaged. State-relative anchoring means the score answers "which Ohio ZIP is most multifamily-friendly in Ohio" without big-coastal-metro distortion. {% if has_mf_data %}
  • Cap rate (30%) — cash flow potential at current rents.
  • % renter-occupied (25%) — bigger tenant pool, lower marketing cost.
  • % multi-unit housing (20%) — existing density signals an active market.
  • Population density (15%) — urban demand pressure.
  • Rent burden (inverted) (10%) — lower share of renters paying 30%+ of income = more stable tenants, fewer evictions.
