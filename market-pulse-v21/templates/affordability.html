What do "Grant", "Forgivable", "Deferred", etc. mean? ▾
Grant Free money. No repayment ever. The state gives you cash toward your down payment or closing costs — pure win.
Forgivable Loan that converts to a grant after a hold period (typically 5 years of owner-occupancy). Sell or refinance early and you owe some or all of it back, sometimes pro-rated. Effectively free if you stay.
Deferred Silent second loan. No monthly payments, but the balance is due when you sell, refinance, or pay off the first mortgage. Usually low or zero interest. Improves cash flow now, reduces sale proceeds later.
MCC Federal tax credit (not a deduction). Reduces your federal tax bill by a percentage of your mortgage interest each year, for the life of the loan. Stacks on top of grants. Example: 20% MCC on \$19K of yearly mortgage interest = \$3,800 off your federal tax.
Shared Shared-appreciation loan. The state funds part of your down payment in exchange for a slice of your home's appreciation when you sell. Down-payment-free entry, but the house's gains aren't all yours. CA Dream For All is the big one.

Buydown — points vs temporary
Permanent buydown (discount points): pay upfront to lower the rate for the life of the loan. Temporary buydown: a 1–3 year payment subsidy, typically funded by the seller.
Per-point reduction is lender-dependent — ~0.25% is the typical retail benchmark in 2024. Temporary buydown payments are computed on the full loan balance at each year's effective rate (what lenders disclose); the reality involves small amortization-balance adjustments year-over-year.
