What's a Lynch GARP screen? How are these picked?

Peter Lynch popularized GARP — Growth At a Reasonable Price. The headline metric is PEG = P/E ÷ growth rate; PEG < 1.0 means "you're paying less than a point of P/E per point of earnings growth."

This screen is a value-tilt on Lynch's classic — it tightens the price floor (P/E must be < 10, not just PEG < 1.0) so you only see deep-value stalwarts, not richly-priced fast growers. Every company on the list must clear:

  • Market cap > $1B — real businesses, not micro-caps with thin trading
  • P/E < 10 — trailing earnings yield > 10%
  • 3-yr EPS CAGR > 10% — real growth, not a one-quarter blip
  • Debt/Equity < 0.5 — low leverage
  • CapEx / Operating Cash Flow < 0.5 — asset-light, plenty of FCF for buybacks/dividends
  • Listed on NYSE / Nasdaq / AMEX — includes ADRs (foreign companies you can buy on Schwab, Fidelity, Chase)

Buffett moat toggle: ON by default, layers a durability filter on top of Lynch's growth-value framework — keep only companies with 3-yr avg ROE ≥ 15% AND 5+ consecutive years of positive EPS. ROE ≥ 15% is Buffett's most-quoted single number; consecutive positive earnings filters out cyclicals that crater every recession. Toggle off to see all Lynch picks including shorter-term names.
