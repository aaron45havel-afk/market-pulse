How the math works — and what it can't see
  • Expected CAGR = 0.7 × blended revenue CAGR (haircut — growth fades) + margin-trend effect + net buyback yield + dividend yield + valuation drift (P/FCF reverting to its own 10-yr median over 10 yrs, capped ±3%/yr).
  • Gates: 10-yr median ROIC ≥ {{ roic_min|int }}% · revenue up ≥ 60% of years and CAGR ≥ 4% · profits positive ≥ 8/10 yrs · ΣFCF/Σnet-income ≥ 70% (cash-is-real fraud guard) · net debt/EBIT ≤ 3× · capex/OCF ≤ 40% · investable jurisdiction. Financials excluded (different math). Moat isn't a column — a decade of high ROIC + stable margins is the measurable fingerprint of one.
  • Cyclicals are marked by margin volatility; status uses their own margin percentile — peak = wait, trough + gates = opportunity.
  • What it can't see: management quality, insider ownership (no clean free feed), disruption risk, and anything not in filings. China names carry a standing warning (audit verifiability). This surfaces candidates for homework — it is not a buy list. Not financial advice.
